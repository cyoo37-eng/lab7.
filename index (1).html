Scene 01 — Asia

Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo is a city of paradoxes — ancient temples tucked between neon-lit towers, whisper-quiet gardens steps away from the world's busiest pedestrian crossing. With over 13 million residents, it hums with an energy that feels both overwhelming and deeply intimate.

Shibuya Crossing sees up to 3,000 pedestrians cross at once during peak hours — a choreography of urban life unlike anywhere on Earth.

13.9M Population
Scene 02 — Africa

Cairo, Egypt

On the banks of the Nile, Cairo has been a center of civilization for over 5,000 years. The Great Pyramid of Giza — the last of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World — still stands just outside the city, defying time itself.

Today, a sprawling metropolis of over 20 million people stretches across the desert, its minarets and satellite dishes sharing the same skyline.

5,000+ Years of History
Scene 03 — South America

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Sandwiched between mountains and sea, Rio de Janeiro is one of the most visually dramatic cities on Earth. Christ the Redeemer watches over a city of samba, favelas, and some of the world's most famous beaches.

Sugarloaf Mountain rises 396 meters above Guanabara Bay, offering a panorama that has inspired explorers and artists for centuries.

396m Sugarloaf Mountain
Scene 04 — Europe

Oslo, Norway

Nestled at the head of the Oslofjord, Norway's capital blends Scandinavian design with wild Nordic nature. Forests cover two-thirds of the municipality, making it one of the greenest capitals in the world.

Oslo was named the European Green Capital in 2019, and has pledged to become carbon-neutral by 2030 — a bold goal for a city that once relied heavily on oil wealth.

2/3 Covered by Forest
Scene 05 — Oceania

Sydney, Australia

Australia's largest city wraps around one of the world's finest natural harbours. The Sydney Opera House, with its soaring white sails, has become one of the 20th century's most iconic buildings and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

More than 100 beaches lie within the greater Sydney area, from the famous Bondi to quiet coves accessible only by coastal trails.
